CHAPTER 8

Journey To Black Hollow Begins

The landscape changes as Brulgwyn travels:

  • The vibrant greens of the Vale begin to fade into ashen woods and jagged stones.
  • The sky grows dimmer—not from clouds, but as if the sun itself hesitates to shine here.
  • Strange animal bones litter the forest floor, untouched by decay.
    •

    After two days' travel, Brulgwyn reaches the base of the Stoneveil Mountains—their jagged peaks black against a bruised sky.

    Ahead lies a winding mountain pass, flanked by crumbling dwarven statues and a broken stone gate with a single dwarvish word carved deep into it: “Kar’darûm” —“Black Hollow”.

    With a 12, Brulgwyn examines the crumbling statues and the worn inscription with care and determination. While the years have taken their toll, his dwarven instincts

    kick in—this place wasn’t just abandoned… it was sealed.

    Looking closely at the carved word “Kar’darûm”, he notices a faint chisel mark beneath it, not part of the original design—almost like a second message scratched in by a desperate hand.

    The scratched dwarvish reads:

    ““Beware the Hollow Heart. It speaks with many voices.””

    At the base of the gate, partially buried under debris,Brulgwyn spots a dwarven holy symbol—an amulet bearing the mark of Thramdor, god of stone and endurance. It’s old… maybe left behind by a priest or guardian.

    You find: Amulet of Thramdor (Worn)
    Grants +1 to saving throws against fear while worn.

    The warning chills Brulgwyn’s bones. “It speaks with many voices…” What could that mean?

    You’ve found the entrance, unearthed ancient dwarven warnings, and now stand on the edge of the unknown—where whispers cling to stone, and shadows wait just beyond the threshold.

    Your current gear includes:

  • Warhammer (1d8 / 1d10 versatile bludgeoning)
  • Water Skin
  • Elixir of Warding Shadows (1 use)
  • Amulet of Thramdor (+1 to saving throws vs. fear)
    •

    Your fate lies in your hands once more—what's your move, warrior of the mountain?

    Brulgwyn clasps the Amulet of Thramdor around his neck. The cold metal warms against his skin, pulsing with a faint, reassuring energy—as if the stone god himself watches from deep beneath the mountains.

    With a final glance behind, Brulgwyn steps beyond the gate.
