CHAPTER 11

Root Of Withering

Brulgwyn narrows his eyes and approaches the pulsing Root of Withering, examining it carefully under the flickering torchlight. With steady hands, he circles the stone dais, looking for signs of traps, magical runes, or structural weakness.

Though the Root itself is gnarled and veined with dark energy, nothing around it appears trapped or warded in the traditional sense. No glyphs, no arcane symbols, no mechanical triggers.

However… with your Investigation roll of 11, Brulgwyn notices something odd: the stone beneath the Root is cracked—but not from time or pressure.It looks as though the Root forced its way up from below, growing unnaturally into the chamber like a parasite.

There’s a faint thrum of energy in the air, almost like a heartbeat—slow, heavy, and ancient. You suspect the Root is more than just a cursed object. It’s alive in some twisted sense.

You don’t detect a physical trap—but the danger may be spiritual or magical.
The Hollow watches.

Brulgwyn grips the amulet of Thramdor once more and kneels before the twisted, pulsing Root. He bows his head, murmuring a solemn prayer—not to command, but to understand:

““O Hammer-Father, guide my hand… Is this curse born of the gods, or something
fouler still?””

With a total Religion check of 10, Brulgwyn doesn't receive a clear answer—no visions, no divine voice. But in the stillness, he feels a subtle wrongness radiating from the Root. It doesn’t feel like it came from any god of his people—not even one known for wrath.

This Root is not divine... but it has been touched by something beyond mortal reach. A corruption deeper than blight. Not unholy, but forgotten—a remnant of something older than the gods Brulgwyn worships. Perhaps something buried for good reason.

Still, it's clear: the Root is not evil by nature, but it has become a vessel for something foul. Its power may yet be used… or cleansed.

With a roll of 12, Brulgwyn takes a deep breath and steadies his focus, his mind sharpening as he taps into his knowledge of the arcane. He inspects the Root of Withering with careful attention to its magical resonance.

The faint pulsing glow and vibrations emanating from the Root have a distinct magical signature, but not one of simple necromancy or nature magic. Instead, it's a twisted form of primal energy, as if the Root has absorbed the power of decay and rebirth, both at once.

Brulgwyn deduces that the Root is a focal point of cursed magic, but not necessarily evil in its intent. It’s been warped by years of misuse—perhaps originally a symbol of healing, corrupted by some ancient ritual. The Root likely has the power to either heal or kill—its potency lies in its connection to life’s cycle but is currently dominated by death.

The energy within it could be channeled for good, if properly handled, or could cause even greater harm if left unchecked.
The Hollow grows quiet, waiting for Brulgwyn's next move.

With a Medicine roll of 9, Brulgwyn studies the Root of Withering, trying to assess its properties as he would a wounded creature. He presses his hand against the darkened surface, trying to feel for any signs of life or any ability to heal the ill.

However, he feels nothing of typical vitality—no warmth or pulse of life as one would expect from a living organism. Instead, there’s a faint chill, a sense of sapping energy, as though it’s slowly draining the life from its surroundings. The Root doesn’t seem to carry the healing potential he hoped for; instead, it feels like an unnatural force of decay.

Still, Brulgwyn cannot completely rule out that there may be a way to reverse its effects through the right method, such as purification or divine intervention.

his clan had passed down through generations, a prayer for purification and protection.

““Hammer-Father, guide my hands. Restore what has been twisted. Heal the broken and bring light where darkness reigns.””

He draws the amulet close to the Root, focusing all his energy into channeling Thramdor’s power through the amulet and his own spirit. The torchlight flickers, and the air in the chamber grows heavy. The crackling energy of the Root seems to respond, almost as if it’s alive, twisting and fighting against the ritual’s purifying forces.

Brulgwyn's prayer reverberates through the chamber, and slowly, the shadowy tendrils of decay retreat, as though repelled by the divine energy. The pulsing slows, and the air seems to brighten slightly. The Root's dark power is still present, but for now, it has been momentarily cleansed, and the chamber feels less oppressive.

However, it’s clear the Root's curse is not fully gone. It will need a stronger, more potent ritual, possibly even a direct intervention from a high-level cleric or another powerful being to fully rid it of its curse.

For now, Brulgwyn has bought himself some time, and the air feels fresher and less stifling.

Brulgwyn raises his torch and steps slowly around the chamber, letting the flickering light reveal more of the forgotten carvings, cracks, and shadows lingering in the room. Dust hangs in the air like mist, and the faint hum of arcane energy brushes against his senses.

Here's what he observes as he examines the chamber more closely:
  • To the left, behind a cracked column, he finds a faded dwarven mural partially eroded. It depicts a ritual circle with several dwarves gathered around a glowing root—uncorrupted. One figure in the mural holds a chalice, another wields a warhammer similar to Brulgwyn’s.

  • To the right, there’s a stone slab altar with dried remnants of herbs, wax, and ash—evidence that rituals were once performed here, perhaps even purification rites. A small groove in the altar’s surface suggests it was used to channel liquid, likely from a sacred vessel.

  • And just past the altar, nearly hidden behind hanging roots, Brulgwyn notices a narrow passageway partially blocked by rubble. It's just wide enough for a dwarf to squeeze through with effort.

Brulgwyn grips his warhammer tightly, then slings it across his back as he turns to the narrow, rubble-choked passageway. The air here is colder, stiller—as if whatever lies beyond hasn’t been disturbed in centuries. Twisting his shoulders, he begins to pull away the debris, stone by stone.

As Brulgwyn strains against the pile of collapsed stone, a large slab shifts—then slips suddenly. He leaps back just in time to avoid being crushed, but the effort exhausts his arms and leaves the rubble even more tangled than before. A cold breeze creeps through the crack in the stones, carrying with it the scent of ancient earth... and something fouler.

Brulgwyn takes a step back from the blocked passage, steadying his breath and letting his eyes adjust to the dim, flickering light of the torch. He scans the chamber carefully, his dwarven instincts honed from years in the stone halls

Near the left side of the rubble, he notices a discolored patch of stone, slightly sunken compared to the rest of the wall. Upon closer inspection, he sees faint tool marks—a hidden seam, suggesting this isn’t just a pile of collapsed debris… but possibly a concealed stone door that was deliberately sealed.

It’s tight, but with the right mechanism—or enough force—it might open.

Brulgwyn squares his stance, gripping the edges of the hidden door with his calloused fingers. The cold stone resists him, but so few things ever stand long against a determined dwarf.
