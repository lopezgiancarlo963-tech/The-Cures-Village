The Tunnel

Brulgwyn tightens his grip on the Shield of Protection, the familiar weight of responsibility heavy on his shoulders. The runes along the walls throb like a heartbeat as he steps forward, casting shadows that seem to shift and twitch unnaturally.

As he moves deeper into the passage, the tunnel opens into a vast underground sanctum. The glow intensifies, revealing a grand chamber carved into the stone with masterful dwarven craftsmanship. At its center is a crystalline basin, pulsing with radiant blue light. Suspended above it is a vine-wrapped artifact—the true Root of Withering, uncorrupted.

But you’re not alone.

Across the chamber, a withered dwarf-shaped figure emerges from the dark. Its body is tangled with rot and vine, its eyes glowing with greenish decay. It speaks in a guttural, echoing voice:

"You seek to cure them... but the root binds us all. Will you rip it from the earth and break what remains?"

It raises a hand, and the light in the chamber flickers. The air grows colder.

Brulgwyn narrows his eyes, focusing on the creature’s words, body language, and the unnatural way its presence disrupts the sanctum. You rolled for Insight to sense its true intent.

With an Insight check of 16, Brulgwyn studies the strange creature intently—the way its elongated fingers twitch, the hollow resonance of its voice, the slight tremble when it speaks of "exchange."

He senses hesitation, maybe even fear. Whatever this being is, it doesn't want conflict. It's bound to this place, a remnant or guardian of ancient magic, and the offer it presents is real—but not without cost.

The exchange it speaks of is likely a trial of will, spirit, or memory—not just a transaction. It’s testing Brulgwyn’s resolve, measuring whether his desire to save his people is stronger than his fear, his past, or his doubts.

The creature leans closer and speaks once more in a whisper:

"Then step forward, bearer of burden. To claim the cure, offer your truth. One memory, one vow, one sacrifice. Do you accept?"

The moment Brulgwyn utters, “Yes, I accept,” a hush falls over the chamber. The glowing passage behind him dims, and a pale, silver-blue light envelops the creature. Its many eyes shimmer, and the sigils on the walls ignite with ancient dwarven runes.

The creature raises one hand, and three ghostly images swirl into being before Brulgwyn:

  1. A memory — Brulgwyn’s childhood home, nestled deep in the mountains, the faces of his kin warm and alive before the sickness claimed them.

  2. A vow — The moment he stood before his village elders, swearing to find a cure or die trying.

  3. A sacrifice — A vision of Brulgwyn’s own vitality, strength slowly fading, as if aging years in moments.

The creature speaks:

“The path to the cure lies ahead, but the door will only open with one of these:

  1. Relinquish a cherished memory.
  2. Forsake your vow and take a new path.
  3. Surrender a portion of your life force.”

“Choose, Brulgwyn Burrowbranch… What will you give?”

Brulgwyn steadies himself before the ancient spirit, its form flickering like a memory wrapped in mist and light. He speaks with careful words, trying to mask his deeper intentions beneath a veil of calm sincerity.

The spirit’s expression flickers—not angered, but not entirely convinced either.

““You speak with purpose… but your words lack the weight of full truth,” the guardian murmurs, its voice like wind through stone. “Still… I sense no malice in your heart. You walk the edge of shadow and light, warrior. Speak clearly—what is it you truly seek?””

It seems the deception wasn’t fully successful, but your peaceful intentions may have earned you a second chance. You may now try:

  • Persuasion, if Brulgwyn wants to open up and appeal to the guardian’s compassion.

  • Insight, to try and better understand the spirit’s motivations.

  • Or you can say something directly to the guardian.

The spirit’s expression dims, its form growing distant—cold, like frost forming over ancient stone.

““Your heart is heavy, but your voice is uncertain,” the guardian intones. “A cure cannot be granted on uncertain intentions alone. You must know your purpose… and stand by it.””

The chamber grows quieter. The path is not closed, but the spirit clearly will not help unless Brulgwyn can prove his resolve in another way.
