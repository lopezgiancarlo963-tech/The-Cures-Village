CHAPTER 4

Root of Withering

With an 18 on History, Brulgwyn digs deep into his knowledge, recalling old dwarven legends and village stories passed down through the elders.

There was a tale, one that seemed like mere superstition in his youth—a warning about a blight that once spread through dwarven settlements centuries ago. It wasn’t caused by sickness, but by a forbidden magic, tied to a lost artifact known as the Root of Withering.

The legend spoke of a cursed root, twisted by dark forces, which drained the life from those who dwelled near it. Villages that came into contact with it suffered a strange affliction, their bodies weakening, their veins turning black—just like what Brulgwyn saw in his people.

The only known way to break the curse was to destroy the artifact or seek the magic of an ancient healer—a rare individual capable of purging such corruption.

Looking at Eldrin’s book again, Brulgwyn now recognizes some of the symbols and descriptions—they match the old stories exactly.

Eldrin wasn’t lying. This wasn’t a natural disease.

Brulgwyn now knows the curse could be tied to a magical source, possibly the Root of Withering, or something similar.

Brulgwyn’s eyes narrow, his grip tightening on the edge of the table. He leans forward, his deep voice rumbling with authority.

“If this is a curse and not some natural sickness… then someone must’ve brought it upon my people.” His stare locks onto Eldrin. “Who is responsible?”

Eldrin closes the book with a slow, deliberate motion. His gaze meets Brulgwyn’s, unreadable yet calm. “That is the question that truly matters, isn’t it?” He lets out a quiet breath. “Curses do not appear without cause. Someone—or something—set this blight upon your village.”

He steps toward the shelves, pulling down another, older tome. Dust scatters as he flips through fragile pages. “If your people suffer from a curse like the one described here, then it could have only come from a source of power. A relic… or a wielder of dark magic.”

His finger stops on a crude drawing of a gnarled, twisted root, its tendrils spreading like veins. The Root of Withering.“There is one place such an object could have been unearthed.” He looks up at Brulgwyn. “The Ruins of Black Hollow.”

The name stirs something in Brulgwyn’s memory. Black Hollow was an abandoned dwarven settlement long lost to history, said to be swallowed by shadow centuries ago

Eldrin closes the book. “If you wish to find who is responsible, you must start there.”
